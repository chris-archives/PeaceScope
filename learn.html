In Philadelphia, gunshots echo like desperate cries for change. Lives are cut short, dreams shattered, and families left grieving. It’s not just a statistic; it’s a plea for a safer tomorrow. The following images are from the Souls Shot: Portraits of Victims of Gun Violence exhibition in Philadelphia. These images illustrate the souls behind the statistics, reminding us that each victim was more than just a number.

Despite the prevalence of gun violence in Philadelphia, there are numerous organizations and resources available to support those seeking help. These initiatives work tirelessly to address the impact of gun violence on individuals, families, and communities. Here are some notable organizations: N.E.E.M (Never Ending E-Motions) The Soul Box Project The Philadelphia Obituary Project Philadelphia CeaseFire Penn Center for Youth and Family Trauma Response and Recovery (CYFTRR) The Trace EMIR (Every Murder is Real) Healing Center The Charles Foundation

Remember, addressing gun violence requires the commitment of every citizen. Let us stand together, advocate for change, and work towards a safer and more resilient Philadelphia
